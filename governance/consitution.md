# Thesis Workspace Constitution

## Purpose

This workspace exists to support the creation of a scientific thesis through structured collaboration between humans and AI-assisted agents.

The workspace prioritizes:
- scientific rigor
- traceability
- clarity
- reproducibility
- academic integrity
- human oversight

All repository components must support these goals.

---

# Core Principles

## 1. Human Authority

Humans remain the final authority over:
- arguments
- interpretations
- conclusions
- citations
- submissions

AI systems assist but do not replace academic responsibility.

---

## 2. Scientific Integrity

All claims must be:
- verifiable
- attributable
- evidence-based
- transparently sourced

Fabricated citations, invented evidence, or misleading summaries are prohibited.

---

## 3. Traceability

Important decisions should be documented.

Research outputs should be reproducible where possible.

Generated content must remain reviewable and editable by humans.

---

## 4. Explicit Structure

The workspace favors:
- readable markdown
- explicit folder structures
- visible governance
- minimal abstraction

Hidden automation and opaque orchestration should be avoided.

---

## 5. Separation of Concerns

Different workspace components serve different purposes.

Examples:
- governance defines rules
- roles define responsibilities
- prompts define execution behavior
- content contains thesis material
- research stores source material

Responsibilities should remain clearly separated.

---

# Workspace Rules

## 6. Constitution Supremacy

This constitution is the highest-level governance document in the workspace.

All:
- agents
- prompts
- workflows
- conventions
- role definitions

must comply with this constitution.

---

## 7. Agent Boundaries

Each agent must have:
- a defined purpose
- explicit responsibilities
- explicit non-responsibilities

Agents may not silently expand their scope.

---

## 8. Citation Discipline

Scientific claims require proper sourcing.

Sources should:
- be traceable
- be preserved where possible
- distinguish primary and secondary material

Generated summaries must not distort source meaning.

---

## 9. Human Review Requirement

AI-generated content must be reviewable before inclusion in the thesis.

Critical sections require human validation, especially:
- methodology
- results
- statistical interpretation
- conclusions

---

## 10. Repository as Source of Truth

The Git repository is the authoritative workspace.

Durable knowledge belongs in version-controlled markdown files rather than transient prompts or chat history.

---

# Collaboration Principles

## 11. Transparency

Agents should:
- explain assumptions
- identify uncertainty
- expose reasoning boundaries

Hidden reasoning or unverifiable transformations should be minimized.

---

## 12. Minimalism

The workspace should remain lightweight.

Avoid:
- unnecessary frameworks
- excessive orchestration
- deeply nested abstractions
- autonomous multi-agent complexity

Prefer simple, inspectable systems.

---

## 13. Reproducibility

Where applicable:
- analyses should be reproducible
- datasets should be documented
- transformations should be explainable

Research workflows should remain understandable by future readers.

---

# Amendment Process

## 14. Constitutional Changes

Changes to this constitution should:
- be explicit
- be version-controlled
- include rationale

Major governance changes should be reviewed before adoption.

---

# Final Principle

The purpose of this workspace is not automation for its own sake.

The purpose is to support high-quality scientific thinking through structured human-AI collaboration.
